“Revenue” on screen = estimatedIncome — fee income net of pass-through media spend. Never scraped; no registry publishes it. It arrives by three roads.

1 · sheet-entered (2019–24)Hand-analysed as Spėjamos pajamos, imported. The bulk.
2 · carried forward (2025)estimate_2025.py: revenue × feeRatio_brand, feeRatio = mean of brand's own last 3 years of EI/revenue. Fallback 0.60.
3 · absentRekvizitai imports get null and stay null. Nothing back-fills.
The Events hole. Coverage is per-brand and lands unevenly: BTL 5/5, PA 5/5, Digital media 42/43, Kūryba 30/33 — Renginiai (Events) 1/10. Only Partizanas was in the analysed sheet; the other nine came via import_rek_companies.py as null. Every Events revenue chart plots one company. Market-wide: 19 of 132 brands have no revenue.

The implicit formula already in the data

Reverse-solved over the 554 rows with all inputs, the sheet's numbers fit one rule tightly (IQR 1.325–1.401):

estimatedIncome ≈ salaryCosts × 1.39 + profit ÷ 0.84 salaryCosts = employees × avgSalary × 12 (exact across 2019–24). 0.84 grosses net profit back over the 16% corporate tax — the sheet's own Fiks ir pelno mokestis column.

The proposed formula is the same shape, two adjustments — a 1.0177 payroll-tax uplift, and OPEX 1.39 → 1.43:

revenue ≈ (employees × bruto × 12 × 1.0177) × 1.43 + profit ÷ 0.84 Net effect on existing rows: +3% (1.43 × 1.0177 ÷ 1.39). Reproduces Partizanas 2019–24 within 3–4%. 2025 is the exception: stored €2.85M came from road 2, formula gives €1.36M — headcount fell, turnover didn't.

Fee ratio is company-specific and wide — media resellers ~0.10–0.14 (BPN, Dentsu), creative ~0.59, most non-media 1.00. Hence no market-wide ratio.

Three different reducers, and two of them disagree about nulls.

AggregateWhereRule
market totalmetrics.ts:16Plain sum over the year. null counts as 0 — a market revenue total silently excludes the 19 null brands rather than flagging them.
segment totalsegments.ts:146Sum of positive values only; nulls dropped, not zeroed. null if no usable row.
segment per-companysegments.ts:145Median, not mean. With Events, that median is one company.
segment per-employeesegments.ts:141Median of value ÷ employees; only rows with >2 employees, only if ≥3 survive. Else null.
segment bandsegments.ts:150Percentile per company; <4 companies → null.
medianSalarymetrics.ts:35Median avgSalary, ignoring ≤ €500 as implausible.
marginmetrics.ts:86profit / estimatedIncome, suppressed when fee base ≤ €50k.
rankOfmetrics.ts:60Non-reporters excluded, not zeroed — “of the 118 who filed”, not “of all 132”.
cagrmetrics.ts:29Compound rate between two totals; null if base ≤ 0.
Consequence: a segment's total and the market total it rolls into are computed on different denominators. Worth unifying if the formula is applied.
